# Copy Research — Comparative Analysis (July 2026)

Three-lane study of live print-site copy, captured 2026-07-20: indie craft shops
(Barrel Maker, Mama's Sauce, Hammerpress, Industry Print Shop, Trust Printshop, Hatch
Show Print), volume leaders (MOO, Mixam, Smartpress, Pixartprinting, VistaPrint,
Jukebox), and the Brampton/GTA local market (Ron Russ, CanPrint, Golumbia, Print2Go,
TPH, Mic Shamz, G Designers, Print Three, One Stop). Feeds the copy overhaul of the
Direction F pages. Companion to `research-findings.md` (market/SEO research).

## What the best copy does, everywhere

1. **Specificity is the persuasion engine.** "Before 2pm EST Mon–Fri", "50 cards for
   $13", "Since 1879", "1-hour rush". Numbers, cutoffs, materials and proper nouns do
   the work adjectives can't. Nobody good says "high quality" without grounding it.
2. **Outcome-first heroes.** Best-in-set: Trust Printshop's "Your custom t-shirts
   deserve to be worn. Because your message is too important to get lost in the back
   of the closet." Speaks to what the print does for the buyer, never the equipment.
3. **Name the fear, remove it.** Print's #1 anxiety is "my file will come out wrong
   and I'll pay for it." Jukebox: "Real experts review your files... so there are no
   surprises." Mixam, CanPrint, Ron Russ all lead with file review / proof approval.
4. **Guarantees as human promises, not policies.** Jukebox: "If something isn't
   right, we'll fix it. When needed, that means a reprint." First person, outcome
   stated. Never a bare "satisfaction guaranteed" badge.
5. **Short-long rhythm and the pattern of three.** "Create. Print. Share." / "You
   need it. We print it. You love it." Punchy fragment, then one working sentence.
6. **Task-shaped, low-commitment CTAs.** "Send a File", "Get an Instant Quote",
   "Start Making", "Try for $15". Never "Submit" or bare "Contact Us"; never
   "Learn More" as a primary.
7. **Sell the touch.** Print is tactile: "Seeing (and feeling) is believing" (Mixam),
   "no commitment, just confidence" (VistaPrint samples). Sample/see-it copy converts.
8. **One craft wink maximum per page.** "FRESH PRINTS DAILY.", "We know your type."
   Always a printing pun, never random humor, never on wedding pages.

## What the local market gets wrong (our openings)

1. **Interchangeable heroes.** Four of nine Brampton-area heroes are the same
   sentence rearranged ("HIGH-QUALITY PRINTS. AFFORDABLE PRICES." etc). Nobody names
   Brampton or a proof point in the headline.
2. **Hedged turnaround.** Only Golumbia commits to numbers (24–72h, 1-hour rush).
   TPH archetype hedge: "we'll do whatever we can to support you in a rush."
3. **Pricing silence.** Only CanPrint shows prices. Everyone else hides behind
   "Request a Quote", which reads as expensive-and-slow.
4. **Broken CTAs.** "Learn More"/"Read More" everywhere; Ron Russ has three spellings
   of its quote CTA; G Designers ships "SEnd MEssage"; the page RANKING for "wedding
   cards printing Brampton" (Mic Shamz) has NO CTA and its entire copy is one
   lowercase run-on sentence with "Cheap" in the SEO title.
5. **The shaadi middle is empty.** G Designers owns luxury (and proves community
   naming works: "Punjabi, Indian, south asian, and Islamic wedding invitations")
   but shows no prices or timelines; Mic Shamz owns the bottom with broken copy.
   Nobody occupies "beautiful shaadi cards, honest prices, fast, in Brampton."
6. **Basic professional English is itself a differentiator** at the local tier
   (missing commas, broken parallelism, name-repeated-in-headline all common).

## What would feel wrong at our scale (do NOT borrow)

- Big-number social proof ("75 million+ customers") — substitute specific
  relationships: years in Brampton, named local jobs, the owner's guarantee.
- Promo-code machinery, seasonal campaign heroes, ALL-CAPS offer CTAs.
- Platform-speak ("Marketing on Demand Platform™").
- MOO-level whimsy; a light wink max, business sections only.
- Any superlative we can't cite ("award-winning", "loved worldwide").

## Voice rules adopted for AZ Printing (v1, proposed 2026-07-20)

1. **Every claim carries a number, a name or a material.** If it has none, cut it or
   mark [NEEDS FACT].
2. **Short-long rhythm.** Lead with a fragment or short claim, follow with one plain
   working sentence. No breathless single-sentence paragraphs.
3. **Name the fear, then remove it.** File errors, paying twice, missed deadlines,
   call centres. Say the fear plainly; answer it concretely.
4. **Warmth is people and places, not chattiness.** Ray Lawson Blvd, the family, the
   counter, the languages. No conversational asides, no trailing-off constructions,
   no em-dashes (house rule).
5. **Task-shaped CTAs, one wording each.** "Get a Free Quote", "WhatsApp us your
   file", "See card samples". Never "Learn More"/"Submit".
6. **Occasion voice for weddings, trade voice for business.** Wedding copy is
   organized around moments (shaadi, nikkah, mehndi, walima) and touch; the one
   permitted craft wink lives on business sections only.
