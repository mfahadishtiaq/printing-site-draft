# Copy Deck — AZ Printing and Signs

Owner: web-copywriter. Started 2026-07-21 during the page-by-page copy overhaul of
the Direction F demo. Research base: `copy-research-2026-07.md`. Placeholders are
marked; nothing invented ships as fact.

## Voice rules (v1)

1. Every claim carries a number, a name or a material; otherwise cut it or mark
   [NEEDS FACT].
2. Short-long rhythm: a punchy fragment or short claim, then one plain working
   sentence. No breathless single-sentence paragraphs.
3. Name the fear, then remove it (file errors, paying twice, missed deadlines,
   call centres).
4. Warmth is people and places (Ray Lawson Blvd, the family, the counter, the
   languages), never chattiness. No conversational asides. No em-dashes.
5. Task-shaped CTAs, one wording each. Never "Learn More" or "Submit".
6. Occasion voice for weddings (shaadi, nikkah, mehndi, walima; touch and moments),
   trade voice for business sections.
7. **Fahad's standing direction (2026-07-21): section headers are simple and clear,
   never clever.** Cleverness is reserved for the hero at most. "Recent projects"
   beats "What left the shop this month"; "Frequently asked questions" beats
   "Before you ask".
8. **No guarantees the shop cannot back (Fahad, 2026-07-22).** A free-reprint or
   satisfaction promise cannot stand. Write process ("we check it", "we show you
   first"), never remedy ("we'll reprint it", "money back", "satisfaction
   guaranteed").
9. **Nothing claimed in the hero is repeated as a reason (Fahad, 2026-07-22).**
   The hero owns the languages claim, so why-us must not restate it. Every one
   of the four reasons has to earn its slot with a different argument.
12. **Semi-professional register, not casual (Fahad, 2026-07-22).** The shop is
    warm but it is a business. Cut chatty constructions ("whenever you like",
    "if that's easier", "even a photo from your phone") in favour of measured,
    complete sentences that still sound like a person. Warmth comes from what
    is offered, not from informality.
13. **Say the quote is free, and name WhatsApp as the channel (Fahad,
    2026-07-22).** "Free" appears on the hero button but was missing from the
    body copy; state it where a customer is deciding whether to ask. WhatsApp
    is how customers actually reach this shop, so answers should name it rather
    than saying "send us the details".
11. **No trade jargon (Fahad, 2026-07-22).** Fahad had to ask what "stock"
    meant; customers will not know it either. Use "paper" or "material", never
    "stock". Same test for any other trade word (bleed, GSM, coroplast, DTF):
    if a walk-in customer would not use it, either cut it or explain it in the
    same sentence. "Bleed" survives in why-us 01 only because it sits in a list
    of things going wrong with a file, where the meaning is inferable.
10. **No negative words, even in the customer's favour (Fahad, 2026-07-22).**
    "Chasing", "blame", "mistake", "problem", "waiting" put the reader inside
    the bad experience while we are claiming to prevent it. State the benefit
    positively instead: "one visit, one point of contact", not "nobody to chase
    and nobody to blame". Applies site-wide, every page.

**FAQ** (question set settled 2026-07-22; answers still to write)
- Eyebrow: Questions · H2: Frequently asked questions.
- SIX questions, chosen by Fahad option-by-option, ordered along the customer's
  own journey from "can I afford this" to "I'm standing in the shop":
  1. What are your prices?
     (Fahad picked "How much are your prices?"; corrected to "What are your
     prices?" — prices *are* the amount, so "how much are" is redundant. Local
     research: correct professional English is itself a differentiator here.)
  2. How soon can it be ready?
  3. Can you design it for me?
  4. Can I see it before you print it?
  5. Do you deliver, or do I pick it up?
  6. Where are you, and can I just walk in?
- Standing rule for this section: questions are written in the CUSTOMER's voice
  ("Can you design it for me?"), never the shop's ("Do you require artwork?").
  That is also how people phrase searches, so it doubles as SEO.
- Deliberately NOT asked, because the rest of the page already covers them:
  file checking (why-us 01) and what file format to send (same). Payment and
  minimum order were dropped to the contact page; they matter after the
  decision, not during it.
- BUILD NOTE for frontend-dev: mark this section up as FAQPage schema so the
  questions can surface directly in Google results for "printing Brampton".
- ANSWERS (written 2026-07-22, semi-professional register per voice rule 12;
  bracketed values are the only things still owed by the owner):

  1. **What are your prices?**
     "Starting prices for our most common jobs are listed on the rates page,
     from [$X] for business cards. For anything custom, send us the details
     through the quote form below and we'll prepare a free quote."
     - Depends on the rates page existing; hold the link until it is rebuilt.
     - Originally routed to WhatsApp; changed to the on-page quote form after
       Fahad flagged WhatsApp was being pushed too hard against the form.

  2. **When will it be ready?**
     "Most jobs take [X] business days from the moment you approve the
     artwork. If you need it sooner, let us know your date and we'll confirm
     what's possible before you place the order."
     - The clock starts at artwork approval, not at enquiry, which is both
       accurate and protects the shop. Sentence two promises a fast ANSWER,
       never a fast job.

  3. **Can you design it for me?**
     "Yes, we design as well as print. Send us what you have, whether that's a
     photograph, a rough sketch or an existing file, and we'll prepare
     print-ready artwork for your approval."
     - [NEEDS FACT] whether design is charged, and from what price.

  4. **Can I see it before you print it?**
     "Yes. We send you a proof to review, and printing starts only once you've
     approved it."
     - Process only, no remedy promised (voice rule 8). Owner must confirm a
       proof is shown on every job.

  5. **Do you deliver, or do I pick it up?**
     "We deliver across Brampton and Mississauga on orders over [$X].
     Everything else is collected from the shop on Ray Lawson Blvd."

  6. **Where are you, and can I just walk in?**
     "We are located at 499 Ray Lawson Blvd, Unit 24 in Brampton, open
     [hours]. Visitors are welcome during opening hours, and artwork can be
     sent ahead on WhatsApp."
     - The only WhatsApp mention left in the FAQ.

- Construction notes: every answer opens with the actual answer (yes, a number,
  or the address) because FAQ readers scan for a verdict. Outstanding facts are
  three starting prices, standard turnaround, design pricing, delivery
  threshold and opening hours.
