# AZ Printing and Signs — Website & Growth Roadmap
*Drafted 2026-07-19 by web-consultant. Approved direction of travel.*

Client: Fahad's uncle, 65, new owner of AZ Printing and Signs, 499 Ray Lawson Blvd #24, Brampton ON L6Y 4E3. Family-run. Ambition: grow as much as possible — website first, then social media.

Key fact: the old business had NO Google presence. Google Business Profile is a fresh start — no reviews to claim.

## The one strategic insight shaping this roadmap
**The Google Business Profile is the longest-lead-time asset, so it starts first — not after launch.**
Reviews are the local ranking currency (~100 reviews ≈ top 3 in Brampton) and they accumulate over months, not days. A GBP does not need a website to exist. The website can launch in weeks; the review base takes a year. Start the slow clock immediately.

## Phase 0 — Design Reset (DONE except uncle's viewing)
"Ink & Paper" rejected → "warm & human" chosen → 3-direction pass → **"The Corner Press" approved by Fahad** (see docs/design-direction.md). Remaining: uncle sees it before build sign-off.

## Phase 1 — Real Content (in progress)
- Uncle answers questionnaire (docs/client-questions.md) — Parts 1–3 are launch-blocking: exact name styling ("AZ" vs "A-Z"), phone/WhatsApp/email/hours, service list + top-3 money-makers, rough rate list.
- Photos: storefront, inside the shop, 10–20 finished jobs, owner/family shot.
- Decide who answers WhatsApp/quote requests.

## Phase 2 — GBP & Citations (start NOW, no website needed)
- Create Google Business Profile from scratch: exact name, address, hours, category "Print shop", photos, services.
- Verification postcard takes ~1–2 weeks — another reason to start now.
- Review engine from day one: QR-code card at the counter linking to the review form; uncle asks every happy customer. Target 2–3 reviews/week.
- Free citations once GBP is live: Yelp.ca, ThreeBestRated, TrustAnalytica.

## Phase 3 — Rebuild & Launch
- Rebuild the 5 pages on Corner Press tokens (in Claude Code).
- Swap all placeholders for real AZ Printing details.
- Buy domain (~CA$15–20/yr, the only recurring cost) — check .ca availability.
- Hook up quote form (Formspree free tier), test call/WhatsApp/form on a real phone.
- QA: mobile, speed, accessibility. Point GBP "Website" field at the domain.

## Phase 4 — Be Found (weeks 1–8 after launch)
- LocalBusiness schema (almost no Brampton competitor has it).
- City-name title tags; per-service landing pages one at a time, starting with proven keyword gaps: "wedding cards printing Brampton" (held by a dated site), "banner printing Brampton", "best printing services Brampton" (vacated by a closed competitor).

## Phase 5 — Grow (month 2+)
- Social media: the content engine is photographing every interesting job — same photos feed Instagram/Facebook AND the site portfolio AND GBP posts.
- Keep the review flywheel running; Google Ads only after organic + GBP are working.

## Phase 6 — Sell Online
- Tier A: pay-after-quote (Interac e-Transfer / Stripe Payment Links — no code).
- Tier B: Stripe Checkout buttons on fixed-price products.
- Tier C: full configurator/e-commerce (Shopify or custom) — only if volume justifies it.

## Dependencies
- Uncle's questionnaire answers are the critical path to launch.
- Reviews are the critical path to ranking — Phase 2 starts immediately regardless of everything else.
