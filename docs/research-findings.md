## What world-class print sites do
- Hero: one identity headline + single primary CTA ("Get a Quote"). Small shops keep nav minimal (3–5 items).
- Section order: hero → service card grid → portfolio of real work → trust block (reviews, guarantees, turnaround) → process/"why us" → repeat CTA → footer.
- Nobody puts a flat price table on the homepage — "From $X" anchors on service cards, quote form for the rest.
- Conversion trio for local service: click-to-call + WhatsApp click-to-chat + short quote form. Trust above the fold.
- Warm/craft lane benchmarks (our chosen lane): barrelmakerprinting.com, mamas-sauce.com, hammerpress.net — restrained modern layout + human voice + hands-at-work imagery.
- Other benchmarks: moo.com, mixam.com, industryprintshop.com, trustprintshop.com, pixartprinting.co.uk.

## Brampton competitive landscape
- Bar is medium-low. Best independents: ronrussprint.com (WhatsApp + embedded Google reviews + prices), canprintgraphics.com (live pricing), golumbiaprinting.com (top rated, ~100 reviews, cluttered site), print2go.com (chain). Several rankers dated or broken. weprint4u.ca closed permanently — vacated rankings.
- Universal weaknesses to exploit: no pricing transparency, no on-site Google reviews, almost no WhatsApp, no LocalBusiness schema anywhere.
- Rankings won via city-name title tags + per-service landing pages ("banner printing Brampton"). Thin city pages from out-of-town shops rank — tactic proven.

- Common service menu: business cards (UV/foil/luxury), flyers, brochures, banners (vinyl, roll-up), lawn/coroplast signs, stickers, large format, same-day printing, t-shirts (DTF/DTG/embroidery), wedding & shaadi cards (big local category), real-estate marketing, design services.
