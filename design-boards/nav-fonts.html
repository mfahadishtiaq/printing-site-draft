Five ways to set the navigation.

Navigation is interface, not reading — so it doesn't have to use the body font, and usually shouldn't. Small serif text in a nav bar tends to look cramped, which is what's bothering you.

My pick

2 or 3. Number 2 keeps the site to two fonts and makes the nav unmistakably part of the same family. Number 3 is bolder and the most "print shop" thing on the page — it looks like a job ticket, which no competitor in Brampton is doing.

Whichever you pick also sets the buttons and form labels, so the whole interface stays consistent. Give me a number.
