Ten typefaces, same headline, same colours.

Every one of these is free on Google Fonts, so any of them can ship. Judge them by feel, not by name — scroll through, and note the numbers of the two or three that stop you.

The body text stays Inter in all ten. Only the headline font changes, because that's what carries the personality. Number 1 is what Direction D uses now, so it's your baseline.

This page is deliberately black and white — the purple is gone and colour is being chosen separately. Judging type and colour at the same time is how you end up blaming the font for the colour.

How to choose

  1. Squint at the screen. The one that still reads clearly and still has a personality is usually the right one.
  2. Ignore the words. The headline is placeholder — react to the letter shapes.
  3. Picture your uncle's shop sign next to it. Does it fit a family print shop on Ray Lawson Blvd, or does it belong to a different business?
